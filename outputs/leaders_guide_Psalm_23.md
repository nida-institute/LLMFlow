# Psalm 23: A StoryFlow Leader's Guide

**Introduction for Group Leaders**

**Note**: This introduction is for group leaders. Use it to prepare, do not read it to the group.

Imagine standing in the heart of ancient Israel, surrounded by rolling hills and open fields. The air is filled with the soft bleats of sheep, and a gentle breeze carries the scent of fresh grass. Picture a shepherd, staff in hand, guiding his flock to green pastures and still waters. This is the world of Psalm 23, where God is portrayed as the ultimate caretaker, leading and providing for us as a shepherd does for his sheep. For the original audience, this imagery was a vivid reminder of the daily struggle to find sustenance and safety in a harsh landscape.

As you guide your group through this psalm, prepare them for an emotional journey that begins with confidence in divine care, travels through the shadows of fear and danger, and emerges into a scene of abundance and joy. The psalm captures the essence of trust and protection, moving from tranquil pastures to dark valleys, and ultimately to a table of celebration. Each metaphor holds the power to evoke deep feelings of safety, courage, and hope.

Encourage your group to explore these themes and emotions, connecting the ancient past with their own experiences. As participants delve into the text, they’ll witness how God’s presence transforms fear into trust, scarcity into abundance, and temporal assurance into eternal hope.

## The Shepherd’s Care (Psalm 23:1)
*The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want.*

## The Shepherd’s Care (Psalm 23:1)

### Senses (What’s Happening in Psalm 23:1?)

#### As an Observer

- What do you see in the landscape as the shepherd leads the flock through the open fields, and how does this scene demonstrate care and guidance?
- What gentle sounds can be heard in the environment, such as the bleating of sheep or the rustle of grass underfoot, and how do these contribute to the sense of tranquility?
- As you watch the shepherd with the flock, what movements and postures suggest a relationship of trust and protection between them?

#### As the Shepherd

- What does the shepherd see as he guides the flock, and how does he use his staff to keep them safe and together?
- What does the shepherd hear as he walks, and how does the sound of the flock moving together create a rhythm of calm and purpose?
- How does the shepherd feel the ground beneath his feet, and what sensations are present as he leads the flock to new pastures?

#### As Someone Nearby

- If you stand on the edge of the scene, what can you physically sense as the shepherd tends to the flock, and how does the setting evoke a feeling of peace and assurance?
- What movements or interactions do you observe between the shepherd and the sheep that illustrate a bond of care and reliance?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:1?)

1. **The Shepherd’s Role**:
   - In ancient Israel, shepherds were seen as providers and protectors, a role deeply understood by the pastoral society. The metaphor of God as a shepherd in this passage would evoke a sense of being cared for and guided.
   - Imagine yourself as a sheep under the watchful eye of a shepherd. What sounds and sights would make you feel secure and protected?

2. **Provision and Trust**:
   - The phrase "I shall not want" implies a complete trust in the shepherd's ability to provide for every need. This would resonate strongly with the original audience who depended on the land for survival.
   - Picture standing in a lush pasture, knowing you have everything you need. What does this scene look and feel like to you?

3. **Sensory Experience of Pastoral Life**:
   - Pastoral imagery, like green pastures and still waters, would be vivid for the psalm's audience, who lived in an arid region where such abundance was rare.
   - As you close your eyes and imagine this scene, what do you hear and feel around you? How do these sensations add to the feeling of peace and provision?

4. **Emotional Reassurance**:
   - The shepherd metaphor not only conveys physical care but also emotional reassurance. The presence of the shepherd assures safety from predators and unknown dangers.
   - How does the thought of a constant, benevolent presence impact your sense of security in challenging times? What emotions arise when you think of this protection?

5. **Cultural Connection to Nature**:
   - The agrarian culture of the Israelites meant that images of shepherding were not just symbolic but part of daily life, connecting them to their environment and the divine.
   - Visualize the connection between the shepherd, the flock, and the land. How does this interconnectedness shape your understanding of reliance on a higher power for sustenance and safety?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:1?)

1. **Confidence in Divine Care**:
    - How does the image of the Lord as a shepherd evoke feelings of trust and security for the Psalmist?
    - What might this metaphor suggest about the Psalmist’s relationship with God and their deepest needs?

2. **From Need to Fulfillment**:
    - What emotional transformation occurs when the Psalmist declares, “I shall not want”?
    - How does this statement reflect the Psalmist’s journey from a sense of lack to one of contentment and assurance?

3. **Imagery of Protection**:
    - How does the shepherd metaphor contribute to a sense of personal care and protection?
    - In what ways does this imagery enhance the understanding of the Psalmist’s spiritual and emotional journey?

### Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:1?)

1. **Trust and Security**:
    - How do you experience trust and security in your life, and what role does faith play in that?
    - When life feels uncertain, how does the metaphor of the Lord as a shepherd bring you comfort or reassurance?

2. **Guidance and Provision**:
    - In what ways have you felt guided or provided for during challenging times?
    - Can you think of a time when you felt you lacked nothing because of divine or external support?

3. **Personal Relationship with God**:
    - How does the image of God as a personal shepherd influence your understanding of your relationship with the divine?
    - What does it mean to you to have a caretaker in your spiritual journey?

4. **Cultural and Environmental Connection**:
    - How does connecting with nature or your environment help you feel closer to spiritual truths or teachings?
    - What modern-day experiences or professions can you relate to the shepherd's role in ancient times?

3. **Cup Overflows**:
   - The overflowing cup signifies abundance and blessings beyond measure. For the original audience, this imagery would elicit feelings of joy and gratitude, acknowledging God’s generosity and the plentiful life He provides.
   - Visualize holding a cup that is continuously being filled to the brim. What do you hear and feel as it overflows, and what does this abundance mean to you?

4. **Presence of Enemies**:

   - In this scene, how do you perceive your enemies watching as you sit at the table? What sensations and emotions arise from knowing you are under God’s protection?

3. **Covenantal Assurance**:
    - The themes of goodness and mercy reflect God's covenant with Israel, promising His people protection and favor. These concepts are deeply embedded in the cultural understanding of God’s unwavering commitment.

1. **Enduring Presence**:
    - How does the idea of "goodness and mercy" following you throughout your life resonate with your personal experiences of feeling supported or cared for?
    - In what ways have you experienced a sense of dwelling in a place of safety or belonging, and how does this relate to your understanding of God's presence?

2. **Hope and Assurance**:
    - How does the promise of dwelling in the house of the Lord forever shape your perspective on life and eternity?
    - When you think about the future, how does the assurance of divine presence influence your feelings of hope and security?

3. **Divine Pursuit**:
    - The phrase "goodness and mercy shall follow me" suggests a pursuit. Have you ever felt pursued by positive forces or blessings in your life? How did that shape your journey?
    - Can you recall a time when you felt a deep sense of divine love or mercy, and how did that experience impact your faith or outlook?
