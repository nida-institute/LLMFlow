# StoryFlow Leader's Guide: [Your Bible Passage Here]

This guide helps you lead a StoryFlow Bible Study. StoryFlow works like watching a movie - we look at scenes from the Bible and go through these simple steps:

- **Step 1: Senses (What's Happening?)** Put yourself in the scene. What do you see, hear, and feel? Imagine being there as an observer. Try seeing it through different people's eyes. What would they notice?

- **Step 2: Context (What's the Background?)** Think about the world of the story. What was life like for these people? What happened before this scene? How does knowing the background help you understand what's going on?

- **Step 3: Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What's at the Heart for Them?)** Think about what the people in the story might be feeling and thinking. What are they struggling with? How are they growing?

- **Step 4: Connections (What's at the Heart for Us?)** How does this story connect to our own lives? What can we learn that matters to us today?

- **Step 5: Summary (What Do We Call It?)** Give this scene a simple title that captures its main idea. What few words best describe this part of the story?

## Guiding the Conversation

As the leader, your job is to help everyone talk and share together. Keep these notes to yourself - participants don't need handouts. The goal is for everyone to connect with each other, not with paper.

### Tips for Effective Leadership

- **Get to Know the Questions**: Spend time with the questions before your group meets. You don't need to memorize them, but understanding the questions and getting to know them helps you guide the conversation naturally and authentically.

- **Go With the Flow**: Every group is different. Feel free to skip questions that don't fit or add new ones that do. Follow where the conversation leads.

- **Build Community**: Help everyone explore the Bible passage together. Make sure everyone feels comfortable sharing their thoughts.

- **Be Present**: Focus on engaging with your group rather than following the guide perfectly. Your authentic presence and active listening create better discussions than perfect questions.

Using this guide will help you create a meaningful Bible study experience where everyone can participate. Enjoy discovering the story together with your group.

### Leader's Guide for Summarizing Psalm 23

**Leader’s Introduction:** "We have carefully explored each part of Psalm 23, and now it’s time to look at the Psalm as a whole. Let’s connect the themes and emotions we’ve discovered in each verse as we reflect on this passage together."

### **Scene Exploration Questions**

**Scene 1: The Lord as Shepherd (Verses 1-3)**

- How does the imagery of the Lord as a shepherd set the tone for the rest of the Psalm?
- In what ways do phrases like "I shall not want" and "He makes me lie down in green pastures" shape our understanding of God's provision?

**Scene 2: Guidance and Presence in the Valley (Verse 4)**

- How does the shift in setting to the "valley of the shadow of death" impact the overall message of the Psalm?
- What does the presence of God’s rod and staff suggest about His role in our lives during difficult times?

**Scene 3: A Banquet and Overflowing Cup (Verse 5)**

- What emotions are evoked by the imagery of a banquet table prepared in the presence of enemies?
- How does the metaphor of anointing and an overflowing cup deepen our understanding of God’s blessings?

**Scene 4: Goodness, Mercy, and Eternal Dwelling (Verse 6)**

- How do the promises of goodness and mercy following us redefine our daily experience?
- In what ways does the assurance of dwelling in the house of the Lord forever transform our perspective on life?

### **Questions for the Psalm as a Whole**

- Thinking about all the scenes together, how do they collectively portray God's relationship with us?
- What consistent themes or ideas emerge throughout the Psalm, and how do they contribute to its overall message?
- How does the progression from one scene to the next build a narrative of trust and comfort?
- In what ways do the emotions throughout the Psalm—from peace and safety to confidence and joy—shape our understanding of this passage?
- If you were to summarize the message of Psalm 23 in one sentence, what would it be?
- How can the insights from this Psalm influence how we view our own life’s journey with God?

By weaving together the distinct yet interconnected scenes of Psalm 23, participants can appreciate the depth and reassurance this passage offers, encouraging them to reflect on God’s protective and nurturing presence in their own lives.
