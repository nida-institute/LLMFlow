# StoryFlow Leader's Guide: [Replace with Bible Passage]

This guide helps you lead a StoryFlow Bible Study. StoryFlow works like watching a movie - we look at scenes from the Bible and go through these simple steps:

- **Step 1: Senses (What's Happening?)** Put yourself in the scene. What do you see, hear, and feel? Imagine being there as an observer. Try seeing it through different people's eyes. What would they notice?

- **Step 2: Context (What's the Background?)** Think about the world of the story. What was life like for these people? What happened before this scene? How does knowing the background help you understand what's going on?

- **Step 3: Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What's at the Heart for Them?)** Think about what the people in the story might be feeling and thinking. What are they struggling with? How are they growing?

- **Step 4: Connections (What's at the Heart for Us?)** How does this story connect to our own lives? What can we learn that matters to us today?

- **Step 5: Summary (What Do We Call It?)** Give this scene a simple title that captures its main idea. What few words best describe this part of the story?

## Guiding the Conversation

As the leader, your job is to help everyone talk and share together. Keep these notes to yourself - participants don't need handouts. The goal is for everyone to connect with each other, not with paper.

### Tips for Effective Leadership

- **Get to Know the Questions**: Spend time with the questions before your group meets. You don't need to memorize them, but understanding the questions and getting to know them helps you guide the conversation naturally and authentically.

- **Go With the Flow**: Every group is different. Feel free to skip questions that don't fit or add new ones that do. Follow where the conversation leads.

- **Build Community**: Help everyone explore the Bible passage together. Make sure everyone feels comfortable sharing their thoughts.

- **Be Present**: Focus on engaging with your group rather than following the guide perfectly. Your authentic presence and active listening create better discussions than perfect questions.

Using this guide will help you create a meaningful Bible study experience where everyone can participate. Enjoy discovering the story together with your group.

## Summary

### Leader's Guide for Psalm 23

### Scene-by-Scene Reflection

**Scene 1: The Lord as Shepherd (Verses 1-3)**
- What imagery stands out to you in these verses, and why do you think it is used?
- How do the actions of the shepherd in these verses reflect God’s character?
- What emotions do you feel when you imagine the scenes described here?
- How do these verses set the tone for the rest of the passage?

**Scene 2: Walking Through the Valley (Verse 4)**
- How does the tone shift when entering the "valley of the shadow of death"?
- What elements of comfort and reassurance are present in this scene?
- Why is the imagery of the rod and staff significant in this context?
- How do these verses deepen your understanding of God’s presence in difficult times?

**Scene 3: The Table Prepared (Verse 5)**
- What does the image of a table prepared in the presence of enemies evoke?
- How can the act of anointing with oil be seen as a sign of blessing?
- Why do you think the imagery of a cup overflowing is used here?
- What does this scene tell us about God’s provision and care?

**Scene 4: Dwelling in the House of the Lord (Verse 6)**
- How do the themes of goodness and mercy tie this scene to the rest of the passage?
- What emotions are evoked by the idea of dwelling in the house of the Lord forever?
- In what ways does this final scene complement and complete the whole passage?

### Reflection on the Passage as a Whole

- How do the various scenes of Psalm 23 build on one another to tell a unified story?
- What recurring words or themes bring continuity to the passage, and what do they reveal about its core message?
- How do the emotions expressed in the passage transition from beginning to end?
- How does Psalm 23, as a whole, enhance our understanding of God’s nature and His relationship with us?
- If you were to express the main message of Psalm 23 in one sentence, what would it be?
- How can the comforting imagery and promises in this psalm influence your own life and faith journey?
