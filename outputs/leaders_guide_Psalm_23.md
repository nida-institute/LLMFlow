# Psalm 23: A StoryFlow Leader's Guide

**Introduction for Group Leaders**

**Note**: This introduction is for group leaders. Use it to prepare, do not read it to the group.

Imagine standing in the rugged hills of ancient Israel, the sun rising over a landscape dotted with clusters of sheep. A shepherd, vigilant and caring, guides his flock to lush, green pastures and the soothing sound of still waters. This is the world of Psalm 23, a world where the divine presence is as close as the shepherd's gentle touch, and the arid challenges of life are met with divine provision and protection.

As you lead your group through this psalm, prepare them for an emotional and spiritual journey that begins with a profound sense of trust and security in the Shepherd's care. Participants will explore the comforting imagery of green pastures and still waters, experiencing the tranquility that comes from divine abundance. The journey then leads through the shadowy valleys of life, where fear gives way to courage as the divine presence offers assurance even in peril. Finally, the psalm celebrates a reversal of fortunes with a divine banquet, where triumph and vindication are served in the presence of adversaries.

This study will invite participants to feel the emotional movement from vulnerability to exaltation and from temporal assurance to eternal hope. Encourage them to see themselves in the imagery, whether they are seeking solace, courage, joy, or belonging. As they dwell on the psalm's final promise of eternal communion with the divine, they will find an invitation to dwell in the house of the Lord forever, a promise of peace and contentment that transcends the challenges of life.

## The Shepherd Provides and Guides (Psalm 23:1–3)
*The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want. He makes me lie down in green pastures; He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul; He guides me in paths of righteousness for His name’s sake.*

### Imagine the Scene in Vivid Detail

#### As an Observer

- What do you see as you look upon the scene of green pastures? How does the lushness of the grass contrast with the surrounding landscape?
- What sounds accompany the quiet waters? Can you hear the gentle trickle of the stream or the rustling of leaves in the breeze?
- How does the air feel against your skin in this serene setting, and what scents might be carried by the breeze?

#### As a Sheep Being Led by the Shepherd

- As you rest in the green pastures, what sensations do you feel beneath your body? How does the grass cushion you as you lie down?
- When the shepherd guides you beside the still waters, what do you see reflected in the water's surface? How does the sight of the calm water affect you physically?
- As you follow the shepherd along the paths of righteousness, what movements do you notice in your body as you walk on the firm, steady ground?

#### As Someone Nearby

- If you are near the shepherd and the sheep, what do you observe about the interaction between the shepherd and his flock? How do the sheep respond to the gentle guidance?
- What physical presence does the shepherd have as he moves through the scene, and how does his posture convey confidence and care?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:1–3?)

1. **The Shepherd's Care**:
   - In ancient Israel, shepherding was a vital occupation, and the metaphor of God as a shepherd would instantly communicate His role as a provider and protector. The shepherd not only led the flock to food and water but also safeguarded them against predators.
   - What images come to mind when you think of a shepherd leading their flock? What sounds or feelings accompany this scene?

2. **Green Pastures and Still Waters**:
   - The imagery of "green pastures" and "still waters" represents peace and abundance, rare treasures in the arid Near Eastern climate. These elements symbolize divine blessing and favor, offering a contrast to the harshness of the desert.
   - Imagine lying down in a lush, green pasture beside a tranquil stream. What do you see, hear, and feel in this peaceful environment?

3. **Restoration of the Soul**:
   - The phrase "He restores my soul" suggests a renewal that is both physical and spiritual. The Hebrew verb for "restore" also implies a return to a previous state of well-being, akin to being refreshed or revived.
   - How might it feel to experience a deep sense of renewal or rejuvenation? What physical sensations or emotions might you associate with this restoration?

4. **Guidance in Righteous Paths**:
   - The imagery of being guided "in paths of righteousness" for God's name's sake emphasizes a journey aligned with divine purpose. It reflects a moral and ethical journey under God's guidance, ensuring the well-being of the traveler.
   - Picture yourself walking along a path that feels safe and purposeful. What emotions or thoughts arise as you consider being guided on such a journey?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:1–3?)

1. **Confidence in the Shepherd**:
    - How does the declaration "The Lord is my shepherd" shape the Psalmist’s sense of trust and security? 
    - What emotions are evoked by the imagery of "green pastures" and "quiet waters," and how do they reflect the Psalmist’s relationship with God?

2. **Emotional Movement from Trust to Contentment**:
    - How does the Psalmist’s journey from a basic trust to a peaceful contentment reveal their deepest values and longings?
    - In what ways does this movement from potential need to abundant provision highlight the Psalmist’s dependence on divine care?

3. **Imagery and Assurance**:
    - How does the metaphor of God as a shepherd enhance the emotional tone of calm assurance in the Psalm?
    - What does the imagery of serene and nurturing environments reveal about the Psalmist’s emotional state and spiritual journey?

4. **Multiple Viewpoints**:
    - How might different individuals (e.g., those in grief or under threat) find solace or assurance in the Psalmist’s depiction of God’s guidance and provision?
    - What does the emotional journey of the Psalmist suggest about their relationship with God and their understanding of divine protection and care?

## Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:1–3?)

1. **Trust and Security**:
    - In what ways do you experience a sense of safety and trust in your life? How does the metaphor of God as a shepherd resonate with your personal experiences of being cared for?
    - Can you think of a time when you felt guided or protected in a situation that seemed uncertain? How did that experience shape your understanding of divine guidance?

2. **Peace and Abundance**:
    - Reflect on moments in your life when you felt at peace or experienced abundance. How do these moments compare to the imagery of green pastures and still waters?
    - How do you find tranquility in today’s fast-paced world? What practices help you connect with the sense of calm described in the passage?

3. **Guidance and Restoration**:
    - How do you experience guidance in your daily life, whether through personal reflection, community support, or spiritual practices?
    - When have you felt a sense of renewal or restoration in your life? What role did trust and guidance play in that process?

## Summary (What Do We Call Psalm 23:1–3?)

**Possible Titles**:

- "Guided to Peaceful Pastures"
- "In the Shepherd’s Care"
- "Paths of Tranquil Trust"
- "Restoration in Green Pastures"

## Through the Valley of Darkness (Psalm 23:4)
*Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for You are with me; Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me.*

## Through the Valley of Darkness (Psalm 23:4)

### Imagine the Scene in Vivid Detail

#### As an Observer

- What do you see as the scene transitions from the peaceful pastures to the shadowy valley, and how does the change in light and surroundings affect the atmosphere?
- What sounds accompany the journey through the valley? Do you hear echoes of footsteps or the rustling of an unseen presence in the darkness?
- How does the air feel as it shifts from the warmth of the sunlit pastures to the chill of the shadowed valley, and what physical sensations do you associate with this transition?

#### As the Shepherd

- What does the shepherd see as they lead their flock into the valley, and how does the landscape's stark contrast shape their path?
- What does the shepherd feel physically as they grip their rod and staff, and how does this touch provide reassurance and strength?
- As the shepherd walks, what subtle sounds do they notice from their flock or surrounding environment that signal the need for vigilance and protection?

#### As Someone Nearby

- As you stand near the valley's entrance, what physical sensations do you experience from the shifting shadows and the encroaching darkness?
- What movements or gestures do you observe from the shepherd and flock as they navigate the treacherous terrain, and how do these actions reflect their cautious progress?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **The Shepherd's Role**:
   - In ancient Israel, shepherding was a vital occupation, symbolizing guidance and protection. Listeners of the time would have easily related to the image of God as a shepherd, providing care and safety.
   - How can you imagine feeling if you were one of the sheep under the shepherd's watchful eye?

2. **Green Pastures and Still Waters**:
   - In the arid climate of the Near East, green pastures and still waters represented rare and precious resources, symbolizing divine blessing and sustenance.
   - Picture yourself lying down in lush green pastures next to a calm stream. What do you hear and feel around you?

3. **The Valley of the Shadow of Death**:
   - Valleys often symbolized treacherous paths. The phrase "valley of the shadow of death" evokes a sense of danger and fear, yet it also highlights God's protective presence.
   - Imagine walking through a dark valley. What sensations arise as you move forward, knowing you are not alone?

4. **The Banquet Table**:
   - A table prepared in the presence of enemies signifies honor and abundance, transforming isolation into celebration. This image would resonate with the cultural significance of banquets in ancient times.
   - Visualize a grand banquet table set for you in a place of safety. What emotions does this scene stir in you?

5. **Goodness and Mercy**:
   - The terms "goodness and mercy" encapsulate the idea of ongoing divine favor and loyalty, reflecting God's steadfast love in covenantal relationships.
   - How does it feel to imagine being pursued by goodness and mercy throughout your life?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **Walking Through Darkness**:
    - How do you think the Psalmist feels as they imagine walking through the "valley of the shadow of death"? What emotions might arise in such a dark and threatening place?
    - What do these feelings reveal about the Psalmist’s deepest fears and vulnerabilities? How might these emotions influence their relationship with God in this moment?

2. **Presence of God**:
    - What shift occurs in the Psalmist’s emotions when they acknowledge "You are with me"? How does this awareness of God's presence transform their experience of fear?
    - How does this trust in divine companionship highlight the Psalmist’s values and what they hold most dear?

3. **Symbols of Comfort**:
    - The Psalmist mentions the "rod and staff" as sources of comfort. What emotions might these symbols evoke for someone feeling lost or threatened?
    - In what ways do these symbols of guidance and protection deepen the Psalmist’s sense of security and trust in God?

4. **From Fear to Assurance**:
    - How does the transition from fearing evil to feeling comforted reflect the Psalmist’s spiritual journey through uncertainty?
    - What does this emotional movement tell us about the Psalmist’s relationship with God and their understanding of divine care?

## Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **Facing Fears**:
    - What modern "valleys of the shadow of death" do you encounter in your life, and how do they make you feel?
    - How do you find courage to face these fears, and what role does faith play in that process?

2. **Presence in Adversity**:
    - When you are in your darkest moments, what does it mean to you to have someone or something provide comfort and guidance?
    - How does the idea of having a divine presence with you change your perspective on difficult situations?

3. **Symbols of Comfort**:
    - The "rod and staff" symbolize protection and guidance. What are the "rods and staffs" in your life that provide comfort and security?
    - How can recognizing these symbols help you feel more secure in times of trouble?

4. **Emotional Transition**:
    - How does moving from a place of fear to a place of comfort affect your outlook on life?
    - Can you share a personal experience where you felt a shift from anxiety to peace, and what contributed to that change?

## A Table of Abundance (Psalm 23:5)
*You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies. You anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows.*

### Imagine the Scene in Vivid Detail

#### As an Observer
- What does the prepared table look like in the midst of this gathering, and how does its presence contrast with the surrounding environment?
- What sounds fill the air as the table is set before the individual, even in the presence of adversaries?

#### As the Anointed One
- How does it feel physically to have your head anointed with oil, and what sensations arise from the oil as it touches the skin?
- As you lift the cup that overflows, what do you see and feel in your hands, and how does the abundance affect your senses?

#### As Someone Nearby
- If you are watching this scene unfold, what might you notice about the movement and interaction between the host and the guest at the table?
- How does the atmosphere change as the cup overflows, and what physical signs of joy or satisfaction appear among those present?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **The Banquet Table**:
   - In the ancient Near East, preparing a table before someone was an act of honor and hospitality, especially in the presence of enemies. It conveyed a sense of vindication and divine favor, transforming potential conflict into a scene of celebration and security.
   - What might you see, smell, and hear at this banquet table, and how would it feel to sit there with your adversaries nearby?

2. **Anointing with Oil**:
   - Anointing the head with oil was a sign of blessing, honor, and joy in biblical times. It was used in welcoming guests and marked special occasions, symbolizing abundance and delight.
   - Imagine the sensation of oil being poured over your head. How might this act change your sense of self-worth and status in this setting?

3. **Overflowing Cup**:
   - The image of an overflowing cup represents abundance and generosity. In a dry and resource-scarce environment, having more than enough to drink would be a powerful symbol of blessing and provision.
   - Picture yourself holding a cup that overflows. What emotions does this abundance stir in you, and how does it shift your perception of your needs?

4. **Presence of Enemies**:
   - Being honored and provided for in front of enemies would signify triumph and divine protection. Instead of fear or shame, the focus is on God's ability to elevate and sustain.
   - How might the presence of your enemies enhance the sense of safety and honor at this table, and what sounds or sights would emphasize their subdued threat?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **Triumph in the Presence of Adversity**:
    - How might the Psalmist feel as they describe a table prepared in the presence of their enemies? What emotions are evoked by this image of abundance and celebration in the face of opposition?
    - What does this scene suggest about the Psalmist's relationship with God, especially in moments of vulnerability and triumph?

2. **Honor and Blessing**:
    - Consider the significance of being anointed with oil. How might this act of anointing enhance the Psalmist's sense of honor and blessing?
    - How does the overflowing cup symbolize the emotional shift from struggle to victory? What might this abundance mean for the Psalmist’s spiritual state?

3. **Relief and Vindication**:
    - In what ways does the imagery of a feast prepared by God communicate a sense of relief and vindication for the Psalmist?
    - How does this scene reflect the Psalmist’s deepest values and longings, particularly in terms of divine justice and recognition?

## Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **Experiencing Abundance**:
    - When have you felt like your life was overflowing with blessings? How did that change your outlook?
    - How do you experience abundance in your life, even in challenging times?

2. **Facing Adversity with Triumph**:
    - Can you recall a time when you felt honored or vindicated in the presence of those who doubted you? What did that experience teach you?
    - How does the imagery of a table prepared in the presence of enemies resonate with your own experiences of overcoming challenges?

3. **Receiving Honor and Acceptance**:
    - How have you experienced moments of honor or acceptance that felt significant to your personal journey?
    - In what ways do you feel acknowledged and celebrated in your community or spiritual life?

4. **Symbolism of Anointing and Overflow**:
    - What does the idea of being anointed or your cup overflowing mean to you personally?
    - How do moments of divine favor or recognition impact your sense of self-worth and purpose?

## Goodness and Mercy Follow (Psalm 23:6)
*Surely goodness and mercy will follow me all the days of my life, and I will dwell in the house of the LORD forever.*

### Imagine the Scene in Vivid Detail

#### As an Observer
- What do you see as the scene of "goodness and mercy" unfolds, and how does it affect the atmosphere around you?
- How do the sounds of this scene, perhaps a gentle breeze or the rustling of leaves, create a sense of peace and continuity?
- What physical sensations might you feel standing in a place where goodness and mercy are described as following someone all their days?

#### As Someone Dwelling in the House of the LORD
- As you dwell in the house of the LORD forever, what do you see around you that signifies permanence and sanctuary?
- What sounds envelop you in this sacred space, and how do they contribute to a feeling of eternal belonging?
- How does the touch of familiar surroundings or sacred objects reinforce a sense of divine presence and eternal comfort?

#### As Someone Nearby
- If you observe someone with goodness and mercy following them, what physical or visible cues suggest this continuous accompaniment?
- As you witness someone dwelling in the house of the LORD, what signs of contentment and serenity are apparent in their demeanor or actions?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **Goodness and Mercy as Pursuers**:
    - In Psalm 23:6, "goodness and mercy" are depicted as active followers of the psalmist, suggesting that God's benevolence is not passive but actively pursues and supports the individual throughout their life. The original audience would understand this as a reflection of God's covenantal loyalty, emphasizing His unwavering commitment to His people.
    - How might it feel to have goodness and mercy actively following you throughout your life? Imagine what it means to be pursued by such positive forces. 

2. **Dwelling in the House of the LORD**:
    - The phrase "I will dwell in the house of the LORD forever" would resonate deeply with the ancient Israelites as a promise of eternal communion with God. The "house of the LORD" symbolizes a place of divine presence and sanctuary, affirming a sense of perpetual belonging and security.
    - Picture yourself in a place that feels like a sanctuary or safe haven. What emotions arise when you think about dwelling there forever?

3. **Imagery of Eternal Security**:
    - The imagery of dwelling in the house of the LORD forever conveys an assurance of eternal safety and peace. For the original audience, this would bring comfort, contrasting the uncertainties of their daily lives with the promise of God's enduring protection.
    - What sensory details come to mind when you think of a place where you feel completely safe and at peace? What do you see or hear in this place?

4. **Covenantal Loyalty**:
    - The term "mercy" in Psalm 23:6 is derived from the Hebrew word חֶסֶד (chesed), which encompasses steadfast love and covenantal loyalty. This term signifies God's enduring faithfulness to His promises and His people.
    - How do you envision a relationship where loyalty and love are constant and unwavering? What feelings arise when you imagine being part of such a relationship?

5. **Imagery of Pursuit**:
    - The concept of goodness and mercy "following" can be understood as a persistent pursuit by positive forces throughout one's life. This imagery would evoke a sense of reassurance and continuous divine support.
    - Imagine walking through life with goodness and mercy close behind you. How does this change the way you perceive your journey and experiences?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **Confidence in the Shepherd**:
    - How does the Psalmist's declaration of goodness and mercy following them all their life reflect a deep trust in God's care?
    - In what ways might the image of being pursued by goodness and mercy evoke feelings of security and assurance?

2. **Eternal Hope and Belonging**:
    - What emotions are stirred by the idea of dwelling in the house of the Lord forever?
    - How does the promise of eternal communion with God influence the Psalmist's sense of peace and contentment?

3. **Longing for Divine Presence**:
    - How does the desire to remain in God's presence highlight the Psalmist's deepest values and longings?
    - What do these emotions reveal about the Psalmist's relationship with God and their sense of belonging?

## Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **Divine Pursuit**:
    - How do you perceive the idea of "goodness and mercy" following you throughout your life? Can you think of moments when you felt pursued by these qualities?
    - In what ways does this verse encourage you to reflect on God's continuous presence and pursuit in your life?

2. **Eternal Dwelling**:
    - What does the notion of "dwelling in the house of the LORD forever" evoke in you? How do you envision this eternal relationship with the divine?
    - How might this promise of permanence and belonging influence your daily life and decisions?

3. **Life's Journey**:
    - As you reflect on your life's journey, how do you see the themes of goodness and mercy playing out, even amidst challenges?
    - How do you stay mindful of the spiritual assurance this verse offers during times of uncertainty or transition?

4. **Cultural Resonance Today**:
    - How do the ancient images of shepherding and dwelling in the house of the Lord translate into your modern context?
    - What cultural or personal metaphors do you relate to when considering God's guidance and eternal presence?

# Scene List for Psalm 23

## Scene 1: The Shepherd's Care
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:1
- **Text**: "The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want."

In this opening scene, we are introduced to the profound metaphor of God as a shepherd, setting the tone for His intimate care and provision.

## Scene 2: Peaceful Abundance
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:2–3
- **Text**: "He makes me lie down in green pastures; He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul; He guides me in the paths of righteousness for His name's sake."

Here, the imagery shifts to lush pastures and serene waters, evoking a sense of peace and divine sustenance.

## Scene 3: Through the Dark Valley
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:4
- **Text**: "Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for You are with me; Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me."

The mood transitions to one of danger in the valley, yet the focus remains on God's comforting presence and protection.

## Scene 4: The Prepared Table
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:5
- **Text**: "You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; You anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows."

This scene portrays a banquet of honor and abundance, symbolizing divine generosity and victory over adversity.

## Scene 5: Everlasting Goodness
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:6
- **Text**: "Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I will dwell in the house of the Lord forever."

The psalm concludes with an assurance of God's continual goodness and the promise of eternal dwelling in His presence.
