# Psalm 23: A StoryFlow Leader's Guide

**Introduction for Group Leaders**

**Note**: This introduction is for group leaders. Use it to prepare, do not read it to the group.

Imagine yourself standing on the rugged hillsides of ancient Israel, where the air is crisp and the landscape vast. In the distance, you hear the gentle bleating of sheep, echoing across the terrain. A shepherd stands watch, a figure of calm and vigilance, guiding his flock to lush, green pastures and the soothing murmur of still waters. This is the world of Psalm 23, where the familiar and comforting image of God as a shepherd comes to life.

As group leaders, you'll guide participants through the emotional and spiritual journey of Psalm 23, exploring key themes of trust, protection, abundance, and eternal security. The psalm begins with a profound sense of confidence in the divine shepherd, evoking feelings of peace and assurance. As the journey progresses, participants will encounter the shadowy valley, where fear gives way to courage under the protective presence of God. The imagery shifts to a banquet table, symbolizing divine abundance and honor, before culminating in the promise of eternal dwelling with the divine.

Prepare to immerse your group in the vivid and emotionally resonant imagery of Psalm 23, where each verse weaves a tapestry of hope, trust, and divine care. This is an opportunity to open the door for your participants into a world where the presence of God is as real and comforting as the shepherd’s call in the stillness of the ancient hills.

## The Shepherd Provides and Guides (Psalm 23:1-3)
*A Psalm of David. The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want. He makes me lie down in green pastures; He leads me beside quiet waters. He restores my soul; He guides me in paths of righteousness for His name’s sake.*

## Senses (What’s Happening in Psalm 23:1-3?)

#### As David, the Psalmist

- What might David see as he imagines the Lord as his shepherd? Can he visualize the lush green of the pastures, and how does that contrast with the rugged landscapes of his surroundings?
- As he lies down in the imagined green pastures, what sensations might he feel on his skin from the soft grass? How does the gentle rustle of the blades affect his sense of peace?
- What sounds might he hear beside the quiet waters? How does the soft gurgle of the water contribute to the tranquility of the scene?
  
#### As Someone Nearby

- If you are standing in this serene setting, what might you notice about the way the sheep respond to their shepherd’s presence?
- What do you see in the calmness of the water, and how does it reflect the landscape and sky around it?
- How does the sense of contentment and security in this scene affect the atmosphere, and what physical signs of relaxation might you notice in those present?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:1–3?)

1. **Superscription – A Psalm of David**:  
   – The superscription “A Psalm of David” reminds us that David, once a shepherd himself, understood the responsibilities and care required to tend a flock. This background informs his depiction of God as a shepherd, offering insight into divine guidance and provision.  
   – How might the dual identity of David as both shepherd and king influence how you perceive the care and leadership described in this psalm?

2. **The Shepherd’s Role**:  
   – In the ancient world, shepherds were responsible for the well-being of their sheep, leading them to food and water and protecting them from harm. This metaphor in Psalm 23 suggests God’s attentive care and leadership.  
   – Imagine being one of the sheep under such watchful care. What do you see or feel as you follow a shepherd you trust?

3. **Green Pastures**:  
   – The imagery of green pastures would be especially vivid for an agrarian society in a semi-arid climate, symbolizing abundance and rest. Such pastures offered not only food but also a place of rest for the sheep.  
   – Picture yourself lying in a lush, green field. What sounds, sights, or sensations might convey a sense of peace and fulfillment?

4. **Still Waters**:  
   – Calm, still waters represent tranquility and refreshment, essential for survival in a dry climate. This imagery would evoke a sense of peace and provision.  
   – As you stand beside these quiet waters, what do you hear or feel that reinforces a sense of calm and refreshment?

5. **Restoration and Righteous Paths**:  
   – The phrase about God restoring the soul and leading in paths of righteousness emphasizes renewal and guidance. For a community familiar with treacherous paths, this would signify safe and right living.  
   – Consider walking a well-guided path with confidence. How does the certainty of the path affect your journey and what emotions arise as you are led in the right direction?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:1-3?)

1. **[Trust in the Shepherd]**:  
    - How does the Psalmist's declaration, "The LORD is my shepherd," make you feel about their sense of trust and assurance?  
    - What might it reveal about their relationship with God and their understanding of security?

2. **[Contentment and Peace]**:  
    - What emotions arise from the imagery of "green pastures" and "still waters"?  
    - How do these images contribute to a feeling of peace and contentment in the Psalmist's heart?

3. **[Restoration and Guidance]**:  
    - How might the phrase "He restores my soul" resonate with someone seeking renewal?  
    - In what ways does being "guided in paths of righteousness" reflect a deeper spiritual longing or commitment?

4. **[Personal Connection]**:  
    - How does the use of personal pronouns in this passage enhance the sense of intimacy between the Psalmist and God?  
    - What does this intimacy suggest about the emotional and spiritual connection the Psalmist feels?

### Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:1-3?)

1. **Trust in Guidance**:  
   - Think of a time when you felt lost or uncertain. How did the presence or absence of guidance affect you?
   - What does it feel like to have someone reliable leading you through difficult moments?

2. **Experiencing Peace**:  
   - Recall a moment when you experienced deep peace or contentment. What contributed to that feeling?
   - How do you seek or find moments of rest and rejuvenation in your daily life?

3. **Feeling Provided For**:  
   - When have you felt truly provided for, either by someone else or through your own efforts? What emotions did that evoke?
   - How do you respond when you feel your needs are met?

4. **Restoration of the Soul**:  
   - Describe an experience where you felt renewed or refreshed in spirit. What led to that renewal?
   - In what ways do you seek to restore your inner self during challenging times?

### Summary (What Do We Call Psalm 23:1-3?)

**Possible Titles**:

**Leader**: These are suggestions in case the group needs help generating a title. Let them go first.

- "Guided by Quiet Waters"  
- "In the Shepherd's Care"  
- "Green Pastures of Peace"  
- "Paths of Righteousness"

## Through the Valley of Shadows (Psalm 23:4)
*Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for You are with me; Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me.*

## Senses (What’s Happening in Psalm 23:4?)

#### As the Psalmist (The Shepherd)

- As you walk through the valley, what do you see in the shadows around you? How does the light change as you pass through the dark valley?
- What do you hear in the valley? Are there distant echoes or the sound of your own footsteps?
- How does the air feel in the valley? Is it cold and damp as you move through?

#### As Someone Nearby

- If you are nearby the shepherd in the valley, what do you notice about his posture or the way he holds the rod and staff? How does this provide a sense of reassurance?
- What can you observe in the shepherd's actions that convey his confidence and trust as he guides the flock through the valley?
- How do the sheep respond to the shepherd’s presence and guidance? What sounds or movements do they make as they follow him?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **The Dark Valley**:  
   – In ancient times, valleys were often seen as places of danger and uncertainty, where one might face threats from bandits or wild animals. The phrase "valley of the shadow of death" would evoke a sense of peril and the unknown.  
   – Imagine walking through a dark, shadowy valley. What sounds or sensations might you experience that would make you feel vulnerable?

2. **Divine Presence**:  
   – The assurance of "You are with me" reflects the deep trust in God's presence even in dangerous situations. This would provide immense comfort and courage to the original audience, knowing that they are not alone in their struggles.  
   – How does the presence of a trusted companion change the way you feel in a challenging situation?

3. **Rod and Staff**:  
   – The shepherd's tools, a rod and a staff, were essential for protection and guidance. The rod could ward off threats, while the staff would guide and support the sheep, symbolizing both protection and care.  
   – Picture a shepherd using a rod and staff to protect and guide. How do these tools enhance your sense of safety and direction?

4. **Fear No Evil**:  
   – The declaration "I will fear no evil" speaks to a profound confidence and peace that comes from trusting in God's protection. This sentiment would resonate deeply with an audience familiar with the threats of ancient life.  
   – When have you felt a strong sense of peace or confidence in a situation that others might find frightening?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **[Walking Through the Valley]**:  
    - What emotions might the Psalmist be experiencing as they describe walking through "the valley of the shadow of death"?  
    - How does acknowledging fear change when the Psalmist declares, "I will fear no evil"?

2. **[Divine Presence]**:  
    - How does the assurance "for You are with me" transform the Psalmist's emotional state?  
    - What does this presence mean for the Psalmist's sense of security and courage?

3. **[Comfort from Rod and Staff]**:  
    - In what ways might the imagery of "Your rod and Your staff" bring comfort to the Psalmist?  
    - How do these symbols of guidance and protection impact the emotional tone of the passage?

4. **[Facing Danger with Confidence]**:  
    - How does the Psalmist's confidence in the face of danger reveal their relationship with God?  
    - What might this tell us about what the Psalmist deeply values or fears losing?

### Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:4?)

1. **Facing Fear**:  
   - When have you walked through your own "valley of shadows"?  
   - What helped you find courage in those moments?

2. **Seeking Comfort**:  
   - How do you recognize the presence of comfort in challenging times?  
   - What or who has been your "rod and staff" bringing you solace?

3. **Trust in Presence**:  
   - Reflect on a time when you felt watched over or protected.  
   - How did that experience change your perception of the situation?

4. **Overcoming Danger**:  
   - Think about a situation where you faced potential danger.  
   - What gave you the strength to move forward despite the risks?

5. **Finding Assurance**:  
   - How do you reassure yourself when facing uncertainty or fear?  
   - What effect does believing in a supportive presence have on your journey?

### Summary (What Do We Call Psalm 23:4?)

**Possible Titles**:

**Leader**: These are suggestions in case the group needs help generating a title. Let them go first.

- Courage in the Shadows  
- Comfort in the Dark Valley  
- Fearless Journey  
- Presence in Peril  
- Walking Without Fear

## The Prepared Table of Honor (Psalm 23:5)
*You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies. You anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows.*

### Senses (What’s Happening in Psalm 23:5?)

#### As a Guest at the Table

- What do you see on the table prepared for you? How does the abundance of food and drink appear?
- As your head is anointed with oil, what sensations do you feel? How does the oil feel as it touches your skin?
- What do you notice about the cup before you? How does it feel in your hand as it overflows?

#### As Someone Nearby

- What might you hear as the table is set in the presence of your enemies? How does the atmosphere change with this act of honor?
- What do you observe about the guest’s posture or expression as their head is anointed with oil?
- As the cup overflows, what sounds or sights capture your attention? How does this moment of abundance appear to those around?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **The Banquet Table**:  
   – In ancient Israel, the image of a prepared table would signify abundance and divine favor, especially when set in the presence of one’s enemies. It represents a reversal of fortunes, where the host shows honor and protection.  
   – What might it feel like to sit at a table lavishly prepared just for you, knowing that those who wish you harm are powerless to disrupt the peace?

2. **Anointing with Oil**:  
   – Anointing the head with oil was a common practice of hospitality in ancient cultures, symbolizing honor, refreshment, and blessing. It was a gesture used to welcome guests with dignity and care.  
   – How would it feel to receive this gesture of honor and welcome after a long and challenging journey?

3. **Overflowing Cup**:  
   – The image of an overflowing cup suggests abundance and joy, indicating that the host provides not just enough, but more than enough. It reflects a state of generosity and unending provision.  
   – Imagine holding a cup that never seems to empty. What emotions or sensations does that evoke about how well you are being cared for?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **[The Prepared Table]**:  
    - How might the Psalmist feel about having a table prepared in the presence of their enemies?  
    - What emotions could arise from such an unexpected scene of honor and provision amid potential hostility?

2. **[Anointing with Oil]**:  
    - What feelings might the Psalmist experience upon being anointed with oil?  
    - How does this act of anointing contribute to the sense of being esteemed and valued?

3. **[Overflowing Cup]**:  
    - What emotional response might the imagery of an overflowing cup evoke for the Psalmist?  
    - How could this abundance reinforce the Psalmist's sense of divine generosity and favor?

4. **[Presence of Enemies]**:  
    - How might the presence of enemies impact the Psalmist's feelings during this scene of blessing and honor?  
    - In what ways could this setting intensify the emotional experience of vindication or triumph?

### Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:5?)

1. **Experiencing Abundance in Adversity**:  
   - Can you recall a time when you felt supported or uplifted even while facing challenges?  
   - What emotions did you experience when you felt this unexpected abundance or support?

2. **Feeling Honored in Unexpected Places**:  
   - Have you ever been acknowledged or celebrated in a situation where you felt out of place or unwelcome?  
   - How did this recognition or honor change your perception of that situation?

3. **Overflowing Blessings**:  
   - Think of a moment when you felt as though you received more than you expected or needed. What was that like?  
   - How did this abundance affect your outlook on life or your circumstances at that time?

4. **Presence of Enemies**:  
   - How do you cope when you feel surrounded by difficulties or opposition?  
   - What helps you find peace or strength in those moments?

5. **Anointing and Acceptance**:  
   - Reflect on a time when you felt truly accepted or valued by others. What made that experience significant?  
   - How does feeling valued influence your sense of belonging or self-worth?

### Summary (What Do We Call Psalm 23:5?)

**Possible Titles**:

**Leader**: These are suggestions in case the group needs help generating a title. Let them go first.

- "Table of Unexpected Grace"  
- "Overflowing Cups of Honor"  
- "Abundance Amidst Adversity"  
- "A Feast in the Face of Foes"  
- "Anointing of Assurance"

## Dwelling in the LORD's House (Psalm 23:6)
*Surely goodness and mercy will follow me all the days of my life, and I will dwell in the house of the LORD forever.*

## Senses (What’s Happening in Psalm 23:6?)

#### As the Psalmist
- What do they see as they imagine goodness and mercy following them daily? How does this vision unfold before their eyes?
- What might the sound of footsteps or a gentle presence feel like with goodness and mercy as ever-present companions?
- As they think about dwelling in the house of the LORD forever, what physical sensations might they experience? How does the space feel around them?

#### As Someone Nearby
- If you were standing near the Psalmist, what might you observe about their demeanor or posture as they speak of dwelling in the LORD’s house?
- What could you hear in their voice as they express confidence in being pursued by goodness and mercy all their life?
- How does the atmosphere change as they envision a life surrounded by divine presence and care? What shifts in the air or light might you notice?

### Context (What’s the Background in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **Divine Goodness and Mercy**:  
   – In the context of ancient Israel, the concepts of goodness and mercy (חֶסֶד, che-sed) were integral to understanding God's covenantal relationship with His people. This verse promises that these qualities will actively pursue the speaker throughout life.  
   – How might it feel to imagine goodness and mercy as companions following you, ensuring you are never alone or without support?

2. **Dwelling in the LORD's House**:  
   – For the people of ancient Israel, the "house of the LORD" symbolized God's presence and a place of worship and communion with Him. Dwelling there implies a sense of permanence and intimate fellowship with God.  
   – What sensations or emotions might arise from the idea of having a permanent home in a place of divine peace and security?

3. **Eternal Perspective**:  
   – The phrase "forever" extends the promise of God's presence beyond the temporal life, suggesting an eternal communion with God. This would provide profound comfort and hope to the original audience.  
   – How does thinking about eternity change your perspective on daily life and challenges?

### Spiritual and Emotional Journey (What’s at the Heart for Them in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **[Promise of Pursuit]**:  
   - How do you feel when you hear the promise that "goodness and mercy" will follow you all the days of your life?  
   - What might it mean for these qualities to "follow" you, rather than just be present with you?

2. **[Eternal Dwelling]**:  
   - What emotions arise when considering the idea of dwelling in the house of the LORD forever?  
   - How does the promise of eternal security change the way you view your current circumstances?

3. **[Divine Companionship]**:  
   - How does the assurance of God's presence and steadfast love (חֶסֶד, chesed) impact your sense of belonging and security?  
   - In what ways does this promise of ongoing companionship with God speak to your own experiences of feeling alone or vulnerable?

4. **[Hope and Assurance]**:  
   - How does the transition from present care to future certainty affect your understanding of hope?  
   - What does this promise of enduring goodness and mercy reveal about what the Psalmist values most deeply?

5. **[Multiple Viewpoints]**:  
   - How might different people—such as someone in grief or under threat—relate to this promise of divine goodness and mercy?  
   - What can their emotional responses teach us about the depth of this promise in various life situations?

### Connections (What’s at the Heart for Us in Psalm 23:6?)

1. **Feeling Pursued by Goodness and Mercy**:  
    - Have you ever felt like something positive is always just around the corner, following you through life?  
    - What does it feel like to be pursued by goodness rather than fear or worry?

2. **Sense of Belonging and Home**:  
    - Can you recall a time when you felt truly at home, secure, and at peace?  
    - How does the idea of a permanent, safe dwelling place resonate with you?

3. **Enduring Companionship**:  
    - Who in your life provides you with a sense of unwavering support and companionship?  
    - How does knowing someone is always there for you change your outlook on daily life?

4. **Hope for the Future**:  
    - What gives you hope and assurance about the future, even when you face uncertainties?  
    - How do you hold onto this hope in challenging times?

5. **Legacy of Love and Care**:  
    - How do you want to be remembered by those who follow you?  
    - What would it mean for your life to leave a legacy of goodness and mercy?

### Summary (What Do We Call Psalm 23:6?)

**Possible Titles**:

**Leader**: These are suggestions in case the group needs help generating a title. Let them go first.

- "Chased by Kindness"
- "A Forever Home in Peace"
- "Endless Companionship"
- "Hopeful Dwelling"
- "Legacy of Love"

# StoryFlow Leader's Guide: Scene List for Psalm 23

## Scene 1: The Shepherd's Care
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:1–2a
- **Text**: "The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want. He makes me lie down in green pastures."
- **Description**: The psalm opens with the comforting image of God as a shepherd, providing guidance and care. Listeners can envision the lushness and peace of green pastures, setting a tone of security and restfulness.

## Scene 2: Beside Still Waters
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:2b–3
- **Text**: "He leads me beside still waters. He restores my soul."
- **Description**: The imagery continues with the calming presence of still waters, evoking feelings of tranquility and renewal. This scene emphasizes God's restorative power and the peace that comes from divine guidance.

## Scene 3: Through the Dark Valley
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:4
- **Text**: "Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for You are with me; Your rod and Your staff, they comfort me."
- **Description**: The mood shifts to a place of danger and uncertainty, yet the presence of the shepherd assures safety and comfort. This scene highlights trust and courage in the face of fear, underlining the protective nature of divine companionship.

## Scene 4: The Prepared Table
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:5
- **Text**: "You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; You anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows."
- **Description**: The metaphor shifts to hospitality, with a banquet table symbolizing abundance and honor. The overflowing cup captures the richness of divine blessings, contrasting previous threats with joy and fulfillment.

## Scene 5: Dwelling in the House of the Lord
- **Citation**: Psalm 23:6
- **Text**: "Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I will dwell in the house of the Lord forever."
- **Description**: The psalm concludes with a promise of continual divine goodness and a lasting relationship with God. This final scene invites reflection on divine faithfulness and the eternal security found in God's presence.
