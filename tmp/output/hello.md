- **Spanish**

  - Greeting: **¡Hola!**
  - Cultural Note: In many Spanish-speaking countries, greetings are often accompanied by a kiss on the cheek or a warm hug, reflecting the importance of personal connections.

- **Japanese**

  - Greeting: **こんにちは (Konnichiwa)**
  - Cultural Note: In Japan, bowing is a common form of greeting, and the depth of the bow can indicate the level of respect or formality in the interaction.

- **Arabic**

  - Greeting: **السلام عليكم (As-salamu alaykum)**
  - Cultural Note: This greeting means "Peace be upon you" and is often used among Muslims. The response is "وعليكم السلام (Wa alaykum as-salam)," meaning "And upon you be peace."

- **French**

  - Greeting: **Bonjour**
  - Cultural Note: In France, it is customary to greet shopkeepers and others with "Bonjour" when entering a store or establishment, reflecting the value placed on politeness and social etiquette.

- **Russian**

  - Greeting: **Здравствуйте (Zdravstvuyte)**
  - Cultural Note: This formal greeting is used in various contexts, and Russians often value direct eye contact and a firm handshake when meeting someone for the first time.
